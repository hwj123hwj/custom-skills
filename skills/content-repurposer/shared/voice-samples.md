Real openings and closing lines from Anuj's published work. Use these to **calibrate cadence**, not to copy. The goal is for new output to sound like it could have appeared alongside these.

## Cadence Patterns to Recognize

- **Dichotomy opener:** Two parallel short statements that frame a tension (Cognitive vs Moral; logo+price vs soul).
- **Demoting opener:** Reframe an over-hyped idea as smaller than people think ("Treat it as X, not a strategy").
- **Pragmatic-flex opener:** State a specific number + ship outcome that contradicts the conventional choice.

## Title Patterns

- Colloquial qualifier at the end: "Contextual retrieval, honestly"
- Dichotomy: "Moral Surrender — Losing the Why, Not Just the How"
- Compound metaphor: "AI Shrinkflation"
- Action + scope: "Flex & Priority: One Line of Code"

## Structural Habits (from full posts)

- Long-form posts use **numbered section headings** (1. Two Failure Modes, 2. The Research, 3. Where Surrender Shows Up...).
- A "Side By Side" comparison table appears often when there's a dichotomy.
- "Personal Heuristics" or "Structural Moves" section near the end with concrete actions.
- Closing section often titled "The Frame to Leave On" or "The Short Version" — a 3-line distillation.
- Cited authorities: Wharton (Shaw & Nave), MIT, Anthropic, arXiv. Be specific when citing.
